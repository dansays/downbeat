**One-line profile:** straight-ahead, acoustic, melody-forward jazz — bop through post-bop,
cool, modal, swing/big-band, and standards/vocal. Centered on the 1950s–60s golden age and
modern players who work in that tradition. Not avant-garde, not electric/fusion, not smooth.

## Preferred Styles & Eras
_Style tallies across the jazz subset; era inferred from repertoire (NOT pressing year)._

- **Hard Bop (63)** — strongest substyle: Lee Morgan, Dexter Gordon, Blakey, Hubbard
- **Swing / Big Band (48 + 23)** — Ellington, Basie, Webster; classic swing is genuinely liked
- **Bop / Bebop (43)** — Dizzy, Tatum, the Prestige-era sound
- **Post Bop (42)** & **Modal (29)** — Bill Evans, *Kind of Blue*
- **Cool Jazz (40)** — Mulligan, Getz, Chet Baker, West Coast sound
- **Vocal / Standards (25)** — Ella, Sinatra, Krall, Nat Cole tradition
- **Piano trio** — a cross-cutting favorite (Evans, Peterson, Yamamoto, Bill Charlap,
  New York Trio, Vince Guaraldi, Ray Brown). Treat a strong piano-trio billing as a plus.
- **Bossa Nova / Latin Jazz (modest)** — *Getz/Gilberto*, Jazz Samba; melodic Brazilian jazz is welcome
- **Instrument voices that recur:** tenor sax (Webster, Getz, Dexter, Rollins), trumpet
  (Morgan, Chet, Hubbard), guitar (Wes Montgomery, Burrell, Joe Pass), piano trios

**Era center of gravity:** ~1950–1967 acoustic mainstream jazz, plus the swing era. Modern
artists in the collection (Krall, Wynton, JLCO, Charlap, Yamamoto) are all neo-traditional /
straight-ahead — so era is less about decade than about working *in the acoustic tradition*.

## Avoid / Negative Signals
_Inferred from conspicuous absences plus the owner's stated preferences._

- **Electric / fusion-era Miles Davis** — of ~22 Miles titles, all but one are acoustic
  (Prestige bop, *'Round About Midnight*, *Kind of Blue*, Plugged Nickel '65). The electric
  catalog is essentially absent — no *In a Silent Way*, *On the Corner*, *Jack Johnson*,
  *Live-Evil*, *Get Up With It* (a lone *Bitches Brew* is the isolated exception). Owner has
  stated they dislike the electric years. → **Skip electric-Miles tributes / fusion-era programs.**
- **Jazz-fusion & jazz-rock generally** — only ~3 fusion tags in 302 jazz records. → avoid
  fusion, jazz-rock, jam-band jazz.
- **Free jazz / avant-garde / atonal** — virtually absent. Coltrane holdings are early/modal
  (no *Ascension*, *Interstellar Space*, late-period free work); no Ornette Coleman, Albert
  Ayler, Cecil Taylor, Sun Ra. → avoid free improv, "out," noise, fully atonal sets.
- **Smooth jazz / contemporary-pop crossover** — negligible. → avoid smooth jazz, "lite" jazz.
- **Electronic / nu-jazz / acid-jazz / hip-hop-jazz fusions** — absent. → avoid.

## Scoring Guidance
_How `/scan-jazz` should judge a show:_

1. **Strong match** — headliner is a Loved Artist (top/strong tier) OR a clear torchbearer of
   a Preferred Style (hard bop, cool, modal, swing/big-band, bop, standards, piano trio).
2. **Possible match** — unknown artist explicitly billed in a preferred style/lineage
   (e.g. "hard bop quintet," "Great American Songbook," "Oscar Peterson tribute," piano trio).
3. **Skip** — anything hitting an Avoid signal, *even if the artist name is loved* (e.g. an
   "Electric Miles" or "Bitches Brew revisited" program → skip despite loving Miles).
4. When a venue's `Notes` restrict genre (e.g. Blue Note Hollywood = jazz only), apply that
   filter first, before taste scoring.
5. Prefer melody, swing, and acoustic instrumentation. When in doubt between "possible" and
   "skip," lean toward including a tasteful straight-ahead show and note the uncertainty in
   the rationale.
